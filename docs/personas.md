# User Personas

Sanad is designed around four core users. Each persona below represents a real type of person we expect to use the platform.

---

## Persona 1: Noura Al-Otaibi — Permanent Disability Seeker

**Age:** 34
**Location:** Riyadh
**Occupation:** Remote customer service specialist
**Disability:** Visually impaired (low vision since birth)
**Tech comfort:** High — uses screen readers daily, comfortable with apps

### Background
Noura lives alone in an apartment in north Riyadh. She's independent and works remotely, but certain tasks — sorting mail, identifying expired items in her fridge, navigating unfamiliar buildings for medical appointments — require sighted help. Her family lives in Buraidah, three hours away.

### Goals
- Find help for specific tasks without depending on family or friends
- Be matched with helpers who understand visual impairments and don't talk down to her
- Maintain her independence and dignity

### Frustrations
- Existing services treat her like she needs full-time care when she only needs occasional help
- Friends feel guilty saying no, which makes her feel guilty for asking
- Domestic worker platforms aren't trained for accessibility needs

### Quote
> "I don't need someone to take over my life. I just need someone to read me a label."

## Persona 2: Ghadi — Temporary Disability Seeker

**Age:** 22
**Location:** Riyadh (lives away from family in Eastern Province)
**Occupation:** University student / bootcamp participant
**Disability:** Temporary — sprained ankle, immobile for 7–14 days
**Tech comfort:** High — digital native

### Background
Ghadi lives alone in a studio apartment near her university. She sprained her ankle badly and can't walk or drive for at least a week. Her family is hours away, her closest friends are busy with their own lives, and she's already asked them for too many favors. She still needs to eat, attend online classes, and pick up a prescription.

### Goals
- Get short-term help during recovery without burdening friends
- Quick-turnaround help — someone available today, not next week
- Affordable, since this isn't a long-term need

### Frustrations
- Asking the same friend three times in one week feels embarrassing
- Delivery apps cover food but not "pick up my prescription and bring it inside"
- No platform exists for short, one-off help requests

### Quote
> "I just need someone for a week. I don't know who to ask anymore."

*(This persona is based on the real experience that inspired Sanad.)*

## Persona 3: Rimas Al-Shahrani — Authorized Helper

**Age:** 28
**Location:** Riyadh
**Occupation:** Certified caregiver, part-time
**Background:** Holds a Ministry of Health authorization for disability care, completed training in mobility assistance and visual impairment support
**Tech comfort:** Medium — uses WhatsApp, food delivery apps, banking apps

### Background
Rimas trained as a caregiver and worked in a rehabilitation center for two years. She left to pursue a degree but wants flexible part-time income that uses her skills. She cares about doing meaningful work and feels good helping people maintain their independence.

### Goals
- Earn flexible income on her own schedule at a rate she sets herself
- Use her caregiving training in a respected, verified platform
- Build a reputation through ratings she can show future employers
- Get paid reliably without chasing clients for cash

### Frustrations
- Informal caregiving gigs through WhatsApp groups don't verify credentials, so trained helpers compete with anyone
- No platform recognizes her MoH authorization as a credential
- Hard to find clients who specifically need her specialization
- Informal gigs through WhatsApp groups don't verify credentials, AND clients sometimes don't pay
- Hard to find clients who specifically need her specialization at fair rates

### Quote
> "I have the training. I just need a place where it actually counts."

## Persona 4: Dr. Reem Al-Saud — MoH Admin

**Age:** 41
**Location:** Riyadh — Ministry of Health office
**Occupation:** Disability services coordinator
**Tech comfort:** Medium-high

### Background
Reem oversees authorization of caregivers working with people with disabilities. She reviews applications, validates credentials, and ensures helpers meet ministry standards before they're allowed to work with vulnerable populations.

### Goals
- Quickly review helper applications and verify credentials
- Maintain platform integrity by removing bad actors
- Generate reports showing the program's reach and quality

### Frustrations
- Paper-based verification is slow and easy to lose track of
- No way to monitor helper performance after approval
- Hard to spot patterns of complaints across helpers

### Quote
> "Trust is the entire product. If we approve the wrong person, we've failed someone vulnerable."
